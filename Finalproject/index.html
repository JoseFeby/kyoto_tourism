Welcome to Kyoto

Kyoto is a city of history, culture and beauty. It was the capital of Japan for over a thousand years, and it still retains the charm and elegance of its past. Kyoto is home to more than 2,000 temples and shrines, many of which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites. You can also enjoy the natural scenery of Kyoto, such as the cherry blossoms in spring, the autumn leaves in fall, and the snow-covered landscapes in winter. Whether you are interested in art, architecture, cuisine, or spirituality, Kyoto has something for everyone. Explore this website to find out more about the attractions, events, and experiences that await you in Kyoto.

Top Attractions in Kyoto

Fushimi Inari

Fushimi Inari is one of the most iconic and popular attractions in Kyoto. It is a Shinto shrine dedicated to Inari, the god of rice, agriculture and prosperity. The shrine is famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates that form a network of trails leading to the sacred Mount Inari. Along the way, visitors can enjoy the scenic views of Kyoto, the lush forest and the statues of foxes, the messengers of Inari. Fushimi Inari is a must-see for anyone who wants to experience the ancient and spiritual side of Japan.

Kinkakuji Temple

If you are looking for a stunning sight in Kyoto, don't miss Kinkakuji, the Golden Pavilion. This Zen temple is covered in gold leaf and reflects beautifully on the pond below. It was built in the 14th century as a retirement villa for the shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu, and later became a Buddhist temple. You can admire its architecture and gardens, and learn about its history and symbolism. Kinkakuji is one of the most popular attractions in Kyoto and a World Heritage Site.

Sanjusangendo Temple

Sanjusangendo Temple is one of the most impressive sights in Kyoto. It is a Buddhist temple that dates back to 1164 and was rebuilt in 1266 after a fire. The temple is famous for its 1,001 statues of Kannon, the goddess of compassion, who has 11 heads and 1,000 arms to help those in need. The statues are arranged in rows along the 120-meter-long wooden hall, which is the longest in Japan. The temple also features 28 guardian deities, willow trees, and a history of archery contests.

Kiyomizudera

Kiyomizudera Temple is one of the most celebrated temples of Japan and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It was founded in 780 on the site of the Otowa Waterfall, which gives the temple its name, meaning "Pure Water Temple". The temple is famous for its wooden stage that offers spectacular views of Kyoto and the surrounding nature. The temple is dedicated to Kannon, the goddess of mercy, and attracts many visitors who wish to pray for love, longevity and success .

Arashiyama

Arashiyama is a scenic district in the western outskirts of Kyoto, famous for its bamboo grove and natural beauty. It is a popular destination for tourists who want to enjoy the changing seasons, especially the cherry blossoms and fall colors. Arashiyama also has many historical and cultural attractions, such as temples, imperial villas and World Heritage sites. You can explore the area by foot, bicycle or boat, and experience the charm of Arashiyama.

Nijo Castle

If you are visiting Kyoto, don't miss Nijo Castle, a UNESCO world heritage site and the former residence of the Tokugawa shoguns. You can admire the exquisite architecture, paintings, and gardens of the castle, as well as learn about its history and significance. Nijo Castle is also famous for its nightingale floors, which squeak when stepped on to alert the guards of intruders. The castle is open to the public year-round, except for Tuesdays in January, July, August, and December
